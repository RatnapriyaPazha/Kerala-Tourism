Detailed Tour Guide To Kollam


  • A famous port of international spice trade in ancient India
  • Kollam is famous for its varied modern industries including cashew nut processing and research, fisheries, etc.
  • Plains, mountains, lakes, lagoons, and backwaters, forests, farmland and rivers make up the topography of the district

How to reach


Kollam is well connected by road via NH-47, NH-220 and NH-208. There are regular interstate buses that run in this route. All buses ply from major cities like Bangalore, Chennai, Madurai, Pondicherry, and many more. By Rail: Kollam is a major railhead of the south railways where all the major trains stop.Trivandrum International Airport is just 66 Km away from Kollam, which is considered as the most nearest airport,You can easily get regular trains to Kollam from other major cities of the country..

Place to visit


Kollam fascinates you with its multitude of tourist attractions. Kollam has one the best islands for a country boat cruise - the MUNREO THURUTH Kollam has State’s best Mangrove Vegetation - ARIYAMTHENGU Kollam has India’s first planned ECOTOURISM destination - THENMALA Kollam has Kerala’s first ADVENTURE ZONE Centre @ JAYATYU EARTH’S CENTER Kollam, with ASHTAMUDI as the lifeline, offers best WATER SPORTS FACILITY in the state.

Things to do


Amongst the best experience is the backwater tour between Kollam and Alappuzha, which lets you enjoy the rustic beauty of Kerala. Kollam also boasts of scenic beach, which allows travellers to enjoy a lazy day basking in the sun or the thrilling watersports. Shopping in Kollam is also one of the best things to do for touristThe fairs and festival make an important part of Kollam, the city enjoys boat races, crafts fair and elephant festival amongst many other celebrated here.

Ideal Time


Summer (March to May)Summers are a good time to visit the city, as summer comes with its own features, like special dishes from the cuisine, with the climatic conditions enhancing the experience of visiting certain destinations, or the fests or exhibitions that happen annually around this time,Monsoon (June to August),Winter (December to February)
